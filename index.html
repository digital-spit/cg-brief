War Situation
Status: DE-ESCALATING (cautiously)

On April 7-8, Trump announced a 2-week suspension of bombing operations against Iran, contingent on Iran reopening the Strait of Hormuz. Iran agreed to allow limited marine traffic under military coordination for the 2-week window. This is the first mutual de-escalation since the war began in late February.

Pakistan PM Sharif has invited both delegations to Islamabad for Friday April 10 talks. VP Vance expected to lead the US side. Iran's 10-point peace plan has been accepted as a general framework for negotiations. However — the ceasefire is explicitly temporary, not a cessation of hostilities. Both sides maintain the right to resume operations.
